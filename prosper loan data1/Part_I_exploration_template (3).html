What is the structure of your dataset?¶

The Loan data from Prosper has 113,937 rows and 17 columns. Among the rows, there are cells with missing values like in Principal borrowed, closed date, borrower state, debt-to-income ratio, prosper score etc. After cleanining effort, i had 75,486 rows. Among selected columns were strings, float, integer and datetime formats.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

The main feature of interest in the data set is Loan Original Amount and Borrower APR.

Conclusions¶

You can write a summary of the main findings and reflect on the steps taken during the data exploration.

The prosper Loan Data had lots of columns many of which are generally redundant for this analysis. Firstly, i had to identify the dependent variables in Loan Original amount and Borrower APR and that guided me towards the relevant independant variables which saw the number of columns cut down to 17. The Data had quality issues in missing data and wrong format for Loan Origination Year. The refined dataframe for the analysis also was rid of 'Other' category within the employment status. The Size was the largest there there was literally no way to explain the makeup.

The summary of this analysis are as follows

  1. Loans with term of 36 months were the largest.
  2. Loans disbrsed peaked in the year 2013.
  3. Loan amount between 4,000 and 5,000 followed by 8,000 and 16,000 were most requested for.
  4. The Employed borrowers were favoured most.
  5. Loan request above 30,000 were priced below 0.25% Borrower APR
  6. There is a strong correlation between Estimated return and the term of the loan together with the pair of Estimated Return and Borrower APR.
